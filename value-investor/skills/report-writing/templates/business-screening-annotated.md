> **About This Template**: This is the annotated version containing detailed guidance, examples, and requirements for each section. Use this template to understand what content should be included, how to format it properly, and what quality standards must be met. For a minimal scaffold without instructions, see `business-screening-clean.md`.

---

## Instructions for Using This Template

**Purpose**: This template guides creation of the initial business screening report (01-initial-screening.md), the first stage of investment analysis following value investing principles.

**Output Requirements**:
- Clear PASS / INVESTIGATE / FAIL decision
- Comprehensive business model understanding
- Economic moat assessment with quantified evidence
- Industry dynamics analysis
- Preliminary financial observations
- Red flags identification
- All claims cited with specific sources

**How to Use**:
1. Read through each section to understand requirements
2. Follow the structure and content guidance provided
3. Use format examples as models for your own analysis
4. Ensure all evidence is quantified and cited
5. Check anti-examples to avoid common mistakes
6. Verify all 8 sections are complete before finalizing

### Economic Moat Assessment

**CRITICAL SECTION**: This requires specific, quantified evidence with sources. Vague claims are unacceptable.

**Moat Types to Evaluate**:
1. **Brand Power** - Customer loyalty and pricing premium
2. **Network Effects** - Value increases with user base
3. **Switching Costs** - Friction prevents customer departure
4. **Cost Advantages** - Structural cost leadership
5. **Intangible Assets** - Patents, licenses, regulatory barriers

**Evidence Requirements by Moat Type**:

#### Brand Power Evidence:
- Pricing premium % vs. competitors (with source)
- NPS scores or customer satisfaction metrics (with source)
- Brand value rankings (with source)
- Brand loyalty or repeat purchase rates (with source)

#### Network Effects Evidence:
- User/participant count (with source)
- Value metrics scaling with users (with source)
- Network density or engagement data (with source)
- Multi-sided platform dynamics (with source)

#### Switching Costs Evidence:
- Customer retention rate % (with source)
- Estimated switching cost in $ or time (with source)
- Integration depth indicators (with source)
- Customer tenure/longevity data (with source)

#### Cost Advantages Evidence:
- Unit cost vs. peers with % difference (with source)
- Margin comparison to competitors (with source)
- Scale metrics showing advantage (with source)
- Process or asset advantages (with source)

#### Regulatory Barriers Evidence:
- Required licenses or approvals (with source)
- Timeline for approvals (with source)
- Number of competitors able to compete (with source)
- Regulatory moat documentation (with source)

**Format Example**:

```markdown
**MOAT ASSESSMENT: WIDE MOAT (DURABLE)**

#### 1. Ecosystem Lock-In / Switching Costs (PRIMARY MOAT)

**Evidence**:
- **2.0 billion active devices** creating network of owned Apple products (Apple Earnings Call Q4 2025)
- **iPhone customer retention: 98%**, highest in industry vs. Android 80-85% (ACSI Technology Report 2024, Consumer Intelligence Research Partners)
- **Average customer owns 2.8 Apple devices**, creating multi-product lock-in (Apple Earnings Call Q4 2025)
- **iCloud storage: 1.5B+ users**, photos/data create switching barrier (Apple Services disclosure 2025)
- **Estimated switching cost: $1,000-2,500** per customer (device replacement + app repurchase + data migration) (Asymco Analysis 2024)
- **iMessage lock-in**: 88% of US teens use iPhone, group messaging creates social switching cost (Piper Sandler Teen Survey Fall 2024)

**Sustainability**: INCREASING - Services growth (iCloud, Apple Music, App Store purchases) deepens lock-in over time. Every service subscription and device adds friction to switching.

**Threats**:
- Regulation (EU Digital Markets Act forcing App Store openness, RCS messaging interoperability)
- Android ecosystem improvements (Google Photos, cross-device features)
- Cloud-based alternatives reducing local storage dependence

Sources: Apple Inc. Earnings Calls Q4 2025; ACSI Technology Report 2024; Piper Sandler Teen Survey Fall 2024; Asymco.com analysis

#### 2. Brand Power (SECONDARY MOAT)

**Evidence**:
- **30-40% pricing premium** vs. comparable Android devices (iPhone Pro $999 vs. Samsung S24 $799) (IDC Pricing Data Q4 2024)
- **Brand value: $516B**, #1 global brand (Interbrand Best Global Brands 2024)
- **Net Promoter Score: 72** vs. industry average 32 (Satmetrix NPS Benchmarks 2024)
- **Customer satisfaction: 98%** (highest in consumer electronics) (ACSI 2024)
- **Brand loyalty: 92%** plan to repurchase Apple for next smartphone (CIRP Loyalty Survey 2024)
- **Social capital**: iPhone ownership signals status, particularly among younger demographics

**Sustainability**: STABLE - Brand built over decades, reinforced by product quality and ecosystem. Requires continued innovation and avoiding quality missteps.

**Threats**:
- Emerging market price sensitivity (brand less powerful where affordability critical)
- Generational shifts in brand perception
- Quality issues or product failures damaging trust

Sources: Interbrand Best Global Brands 2024; IDC Pricing Analysis Q4 2024; Satmetrix NPS Benchmarks 2024

#### 3. Scale Advantages (TERTIARY)

**Evidence**:
- **R&D leverage**: $31B R&D on $416B revenue (7.8%) enables custom silicon (A-series, M-series chips) that competitors cannot match at similar scale
- **Supply chain power**: Volume enables preferential component pricing and allocation priority during shortages
- **Services platform**: 1B+ paid subscriptions creating $100B+ annual services revenue with 74% gross margins (Apple Services Report FY2025)
- **Retail distribution**: 500+ Apple Stores generating $200B+ annual revenue with premium positioning

**Sustainability**: INCREASING - Scale advantages compound as business grows. Custom silicon investments only feasible at Apple's volume.

**Threats**:
- Scale can breed complacency and slow innovation
- Large organization risk of bureaucracy
- Regulatory scrutiny of market power

Source: Apple Inc. 10-K FY2025

**OVERALL MOAT STRENGTH**: **WIDE AND WIDENING**

**Moat Durability**: 10+ years with high confidence. Ecosystem lock-in strengthens as customers acquire more devices and services. Brand power deeply entrenched. Scale advantages increase with size.

**Primary Risk to Moat**: Regulatory action forcing ecosystem openness (App Store competition, third-party payment systems, RCS messaging). EU Digital Markets Act implementation represents most significant near-term moat threat.
```
