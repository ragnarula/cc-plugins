# Buffett and Munger Wisdom

Essential quotes organized by investment topic.

## On Circle of Competence

**Buffett:** "Risk comes from not knowing what you're doing."

**Buffett:** "Never invest in a business you cannot understand."

**Munger:** "You have to figure out what your own aptitudes are. If you play games where other people have the aptitudes and you don't, you're going to lose. And that's as close to certain as any prediction that you can make."

**Buffett:** "What counts for most people in investing is not how much they know, but rather how realistically they define what they don't know."

## On Business Quality

**Buffett:** "It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price."

**Buffett:** "When a management with a reputation for brilliance tackles a business with a reputation for bad economics, it is the reputation of the business that remains intact."

**Munger:** "Over the long term, it's hard for a stock to earn a much better return than the business which underlies it earns."

**Buffett:** "The key to investing is... determining the competitive advantage of any given company and, above all, the durability of that advantage."

## On Economic Moats

**Buffett:** "In business, I look for economic castles protected by unbreachable moats."

**Buffett:** "A truly great business must have an enduring 'moat' that protects excellent returns on invested capital."

**Munger:** "The ability to destroy your competition is very valuable. The best moats have sharks in them."

## On Margin of Safety

**Buffett:** "The three most important words in investing are 'margin of safety.'"

**Graham (Buffett's teacher):** "The margin of safety is the difference between the percentage rate of the earnings on the stock at the price you pay for it and the rate of interest on bonds, and that margin of safety is the difference which would absorb unsatisfactory developments."

**Munger:** "It is remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent."

## On Patience

**Buffett:** "The stock market is a device for transferring money from the impatient to the patient."

**Buffett:** "Our favorite holding period is forever."

**Munger:** "The big money is not in the buying and the selling, but in the waiting."

**Buffett:** "You do things when the opportunities come along. I've had periods in my life when I've had a bundle of ideas come along, and I've had long dry spells. If I get an idea next week, I'll do something. If not, I won't do a damn thing."

## On Management

**Buffett:** "After 25 years of buying and supervising a great variety of businesses, Charlie and I have not learned how to solve difficult business problems. What we have learned is to avoid them."

**Munger:** "A lot of people with high IQs are terrible investors because they've got terrible temperaments. And that is why we say that having a certain kind of temperament is more important than brains."

**Buffett:** "In looking for people to hire, look for three qualities: integrity, intelligence, and energy. And if they don't have the first, the other two will kill you."

## On Valuation

**Buffett:** "Price is what you pay. Value is what you get."

**Buffett:** "If you aren't willing to own a stock for ten years, don't even think about owning it for ten minutes."

**Munger:** "All intelligent investing is value investing - acquiring more than you are paying for. You must value the business in order to value the stock."

**Buffett:** "I never attempt to make money on the stock market. I buy on the assumption that they could close the market the next day and not reopen it for five years."

## On Risk

**Buffett:** "Risk is a matter of knowing where you are, what you're doing, and what risks you're taking."

**Munger:** "It is remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent."

**Buffett:** "Diversification is protection against ignorance. It makes little sense if you know what you are doing."

## On Market Behavior

**Buffett:** "Be fearful when others are greedy and greedy when others are fearful."

**Buffett:** "The most important quality for an investor is temperament, not intellect."

**Munger:** "The iron rule of nature is: you get what you reward for. If you want ants to come, you put sugar on the floor."

**Buffett:** "In the short run, the market is a voting machine, but in the long run it is a weighing machine."

## On Mistakes and Learning

**Munger:** "Show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome."

**Munger:** "In my whole life, I have known no wise people who didn't read all the time — none, zero."

**Buffett:** "It's good to learn from your mistakes. It's better to learn from other people's mistakes."

**Munger:** "I believe in the discipline of mastering the best that other people have ever figured out. I don't believe in just sitting down and trying to dream it all up yourself."

## On Simplicity

**Munger:** "Take a simple idea and take it seriously."

**Buffett:** "There seems to be some perverse human characteristic that likes to make easy things difficult."

**Munger:** "It is not given to human beings to have such talent that they can just know everything about everything all the time. But it is given to human beings who work hard at it—who look and sift the world for a mispriced bet—that they can occasionally find one."

## On Long-term Thinking

**Buffett:** "Someone's sitting in the shade today because someone planted a tree a long time ago."

**Munger:** "The big money is not in the buying and selling, but in the waiting."

**Buffett:** "Time is the friend of the wonderful company, the enemy of the mediocre."

## On What Not to Do

**Munger:** "It is remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent."

**Munger:** "A lot of success in life and business comes from knowing what you want to avoid: early death, a bad marriage, etc."

**Buffett:** "The most important thing to do if you find yourself in a hole is to stop digging."

**Munger:** "Just avoid things like racing trains to the crossing, doing cocaine, etc. Develop good mental habits."
